# Drowning

Remove the person from the water only if safe to do so. Start CPR immediately if they are unresponsive and not breathing normally — call 000 for every drowning casualty, even if they seem to recover.

## What to do

1. **Call 000** for every drowning casualty.
2. Remove them from the water — **do not endanger yourself**. Throw a rope or flotation device if you cannot safely enter the water.
3. Follow [DRSABCD](/kb/drsabcd).
4. Lay them on their back. If they are not breathing normally, start [CPR](/kb/cpr) immediately — give **5 initial rescue breaths** before starting compressions.
5. Be prepared for vomiting — roll on their side and clear the mouth.
6. Continue CPR until they recover or paramedics arrive.
7. Do **not** try to empty water from the stomach by pressing on the abdomen.

## Secondary drowning

Anyone who has nearly drowned should be assessed at hospital, even if they seem fine. Water in the lungs can cause life-threatening problems hours later.

## Hypothermia

People rescued from cold water are often hypothermic — see [hypothermia](/kb/hypothermia). Continue CPR for longer than usual; resuscitation has succeeded after extended periods in cold water.
