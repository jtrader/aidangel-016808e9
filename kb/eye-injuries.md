# Eye Injuries

For something in the eye, wash out with clean water or saline. Never remove an embedded object — cover both eyes and call 000. Chemical splashes need 20 minutes of running water and an ambulance.

## Foreign body (grit, eyelash)

1. Don't rub the eye.
2. Tilt the head so the affected eye is down.
3. Flush with clean water, saline or eye-wash solution.
4. If it does not come out, cover the eye with a clean pad and seek medical aid.

## Embedded object

1. Do **not** try to remove it.
2. Cover the eye loosely with a paper cup or pad — no pressure.
3. Cover the **other eye too** to stop both eyes moving.
4. **Call 000.**

## Chemical splash

1. **Immediately** flush the eye with cool running water for at least **20 minutes**. Tilt the head so the chemical runs away from the unaffected eye.
2. Hold the eyelids open.
3. **Call 000.**
4. If you know the chemical, take the container with you to hospital.

## Blow to the eye

Apply a cold pack to reduce swelling. Seek medical aid if there is any vision change, severe pain, blood inside the eye, or the eye looks abnormal.
