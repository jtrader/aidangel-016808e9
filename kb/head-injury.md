# Head Injury and Concussion

Always suspect a spinal injury with a head injury. Lay the person down, control bleeding without direct pressure on a depressed area, and call 000 for any loss of consciousness, vomiting or confusion.

## Concerning signs

- Loss of consciousness — even briefly
- Persistent headache, confusion, drowsiness
- Repeated vomiting
- Unequal pupils or vision changes
- Seizures
- Clear fluid or blood from the nose or ears
- Weakness or numbness in any limb

Any of these — **call 000**.

## What to do

1. Follow [DRSABCD](/kb/drsabcd).
2. Always suspect a [spinal injury](/kb/spinal-injury). Keep the head and neck still.
3. **Conscious:** lie them down with the head and shoulders slightly raised. Control any bleeding (avoid pressure on a depressed skull area).
4. **Unconscious but breathing:** place in the [recovery position](/kb/recovery-position), supporting the head and neck.
5. Do not give anything to eat or drink.
6. Stay with them until paramedics arrive.

## Concussion

Anyone who has had loss of consciousness or significant memory loss from a head blow should **not** return to sport, driving or work until cleared by a doctor. Watch for delayed deterioration over the next 24–48 hours.
