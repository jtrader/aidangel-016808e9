# Seizures and Epilepsy

During a seizure, protect the person from injury, time it, and never restrain them or put anything in their mouth. Afterwards, place them in the recovery position and reassure them.

## During a seizure

1. Note the **time** the seizure starts.
2. Move objects away to protect the person from injury.
3. Cushion the head with something soft (jumper, towel).
4. Loosen tight clothing around the neck.
5. **Do NOT** restrain them, hold them down, or put anything in their mouth.
6. Let the seizure run its course.

## After the seizure

1. Follow [DRSABCD](/kb/drsabcd).
2. Place them in the [recovery position](/kb/recovery-position).
3. Manage any injuries.
4. Reassure — they may be confused or drowsy for some time.
5. Stay with them until fully recovered.

## Call 000 if

- The seizure lasts longer than **5 minutes**
- A second seizure follows quickly
- The person does not regain consciousness within 5 minutes
- They are injured, pregnant, diabetic, or it is their first seizure
- The seizure happened in water

## Febrile convulsions (children)

Caused by fever, usually in children 6 months to 6 years.
- Roll the child on their side; do not restrain.
- Remove excess clothing.
- Do NOT cool them by sponging or bathing.
- Seek medical aid afterwards.
