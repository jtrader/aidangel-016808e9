# Snake Bite — Pressure Immobilisation

For any suspected snake bite in Australia, call 000 and apply a pressure immobilisation bandage from the bite site up the entire limb, then splint and keep the patient completely still.

## What to do (assume venomous)

1. **Call 000.**
2. Follow [DRSABCD](/kb/drsabcd).
3. Keep the patient **absolutely still** and lying down. Movement spreads venom.
4. Apply a **pressure immobilisation bandage**:
   - Use a broad heavy-crepe bandage (or torn clothing if needed).
   - Start over the bite site, wrapping firmly — as tight as a sprain bandage.
   - Continue **up the entire limb**, covering as much as possible.
   - Mark the bite site on the outside of the bandage.
5. **Splint** the limb to prevent any movement.
6. Note the time of bite and time bandage applied.
7. Reassure and monitor. Be ready to start [CPR](/kb/cpr).

## Do NOT

- Do **not** wash the bite — venom on the skin helps identify the snake.
- Do **not** cut or suck the wound.
- Do **not** apply a tourniquet.
- Do **not** remove the bandage — only paramedics or hospital staff do this.
- Do **not** try to catch or kill the snake.
