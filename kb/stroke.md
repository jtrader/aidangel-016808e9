# Stroke — The FAST Test

Use the FAST test for stroke — Face drooping, Arms weakness, Speech slurred, Time to call 000. Every minute counts; lay the person down with head slightly raised and give nothing by mouth.

## The FAST test

- **F — Face.** Has their face drooped on one side? Can they smile evenly?
- **A — Arms.** Can they lift both arms and hold them there?
- **S — Speech.** Is their speech slurred? Can they understand you?
- **T — Time.** Time is brain — **call 000 immediately**.

Other signs include sudden numbness or weakness, sudden confusion, sudden vision loss, severe headache, dizziness or loss of balance.

## What to do

1. **Call 000** — note the time symptoms started; this affects treatment.
2. Follow [DRSABCD](/kb/drsabcd).
3. Lie the person down with their head and shoulders slightly raised.
4. Give **nothing to eat or drink** — swallowing may be affected.
5. Reassure them and stay with them.
6. If they become unconscious but are breathing, place them in the [recovery position](/kb/recovery-position).
7. If they stop breathing normally, start [CPR](/kb/cpr).

## TIA (mini-stroke)

If symptoms resolve quickly, it may have been a transient ischaemic attack. Still call 000 — it is a warning sign of a major stroke.
