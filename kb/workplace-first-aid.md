# Workplace-Specific First Aid

Workplaces in Australia must provide first aid appropriate to the hazards present. High-risk industries like construction, hospitality and childcare each have predictable injuries — knowing the common scenarios ahead of time saves precious minutes.

In any serious emergency, follow [DRSABCD](/kb/drsabcd) and call [000](tel:000).

## Construction sites

Common incidents: crush injuries, falls from height, lacerations from power tools, eye injuries from grinding/welding, and electric shock.

- **Crush or impalement:** Do **not** remove the object. Control bleeding around it with pressure pads. See [Bleeding](/kb/bleeding).
- **Falls from height:** Assume [spinal injury](/kb/spinal-injury). Keep the person still; only move if there is immediate danger.
- **Power-tool lacerations:** Apply firm direct pressure; elevate. Bag and ice any amputated part — send with the casualty to hospital.
- **Eye injuries** from metal splinters or grinding dust: cover both eyes, do not rub. See [Eye Injuries](/kb/eye-injuries).
- **Electric shock:** Turn off power before touching the casualty. See [Electric Shock](/kb/electric-shock).
- **Concrete burns:** Wet cement is alkaline — flush with running water for at least 20 minutes. See [Burns](/kb/burns).

A site first aid kit should include large trauma dressings, an eye-wash bottle, and a clearly marked AED if more than 25 workers are on site.

## Hospitality (kitchens, cafes, bars)

Common incidents: scalds and burns, knife cuts, slips on wet floors, choking on food, and anaphylaxis from allergens.

- **Scalds and burns:** 20 minutes under cool running water. See [Burns](/kb/burns).
- **Deep knife cuts:** Direct pressure with a clean cloth; elevate the limb above heart level.
- **Choking guests:** 5 back blows, then 5 chest thrusts. See [Choking](/kb/choking).
- **Anaphylaxis** (nut, shellfish, sesame): Lay flat, give adrenaline auto-injector into outer thigh, call [000](tel:000). See [Anaphylaxis](/kb/anaphylaxis).
- **Alcohol-related collapse:** Recovery position, monitor breathing. See [Recovery Position](/kb/recovery-position).

## Childcare and schools

Common incidents: febrile seizures, asthma attacks, allergic reactions, head bumps from falls, and choking on small objects.

- **Asthma attack:** 4 puffs of blue reliever, wait 4 minutes, repeat. Call [000](tel:000) if no improvement. See [Asthma](/kb/asthma).
- **Anaphylaxis:** Use the child's EpiPen Jr (under 20 kg). See [Anaphylaxis](/kb/anaphylaxis).
- **Seizures:** Time it, protect the head, do not restrain or put anything in the mouth. See [Seizures](/kb/seizures).
- **Choking infants/children:** Different technique to adults — see [Choking](/kb/choking).
- **Head bumps:** Watch for vomiting, drowsiness, unequal pupils. See [Head Injury](/kb/head-injury).
- **Bleeding from baby teeth or knocked-out adult teeth:** See [Dental Injury](/kb/dental-injury).

Childcare staff in Australia must hold HLTAID012 (Provide First Aid in an education and care setting), which includes asthma and anaphylaxis training.

## Workplace obligations

Under WHS law, employers must provide trained first aiders, accessible kits, and clear emergency procedures matched to the hazards of the work. A single generic kit in a drawer is not enough on a building site or in a commercial kitchen.
